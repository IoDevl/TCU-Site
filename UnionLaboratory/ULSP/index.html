UNION'S LABORATORY
SPACE PROGRAM

Aerospace • Cosmos Exploration

The Union's Laboratory Space Program (ULSP) is The Computer Union's dedicated space program, responsible for developing all its rockets, missiles, and satellites. It operates as a subdivision of the Union's Laboratory.

PROGRAM OVERVIEW

The ULSP primarily focuses on exploring the cosmos and the universe. Additionally, it plays a crucial role in establishing communication satellites for the Union and contributing to the Motherland's defense through weapons like the Fanta Missile.

Engineer Otto founded the program in 1945, establishing the foundation for The Computer Union's space exploration and aerospace development capabilities.

The program serves as the primary center for all aerospace-related research and development, including spacecraft design, satellite technology, and advanced missile systems for both defensive and exploratory purposes.

PROGRAM DEPARTMENTS

AEROSPACE ENGINEERING DEPARTMENT

Aerospace Engineers are tasked with the development of all aerospace-related inventions and constitute the majority of the Space Program's personnel. This includes but does not limit to; aircraft, rockets, missiles, ICBMS, bombs (if dropped from aircraft), etc.

Department Head:

Mr. Ludwig currently heads this department.

COSMONAUT DEPARTMENT

Cosmonauts risk their lives exploring the cosmos. They undergo regular training to maintain their skills and physical condition.

Department Head:

This department is currently led by Chief Cosmonaut Jedi.
